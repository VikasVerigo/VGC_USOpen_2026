Tournament Format

How It Works

Three components combine into a single score. Lowest total wins.

01

Pick 2 PGA Players

Choose any two players competing in the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills — excluding the top 5 in the Official World Golf Ranking. Picks lock before the June 17 Men's Night tee-off — no changes after that.

02

Play 2 VGC Rounds

Your net score (gross minus handicap) from two Wednesday Men's Night rounds counts — one the week before the US Open, one the week after.

03

Combine & Compete

Your two net rounds plus your two PGA players' total stroke counts across all four US Open rounds are added together. Lowest combined score wins.

Combined Score Formula
Net Round 1 + Net Round 2
+ PGA Pick 1 Total Strokes + PGA Pick 2 Total Strokes
= Combined Score  (lowest wins)

Net Score = Gross strokes − course handicap for that week

⚠

Missed Cut Penalty: If one of your PGA picks misses the cut (plays only 2 rounds), their score is calculated as actual R1 + R2 + 80 + 80 — 80 strokes assigned for each unplayed round — giving a 4-round total for scoring purposes.
