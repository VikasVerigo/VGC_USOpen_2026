Tournament Format

How It Works

Three components combine into a single score. Lowest total wins.

01

Pick 2 PGA Players

Choose any two players competing in the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills — excluding the top 5 in the Official World Golf Ranking (currently: Scheffler, McIlroy, Cameron Young, Fitzpatrick, Russell Henley). Picks close June 18 at 3am — no changes after that.

02

Play a VGC Wednesday Round

Your best net score (gross minus handicap) from either Wednesday Men's Night round counts — one the week before the US Open (Jun 17) and one the week after (Jun 24). Only your better score is used, so you only need to play one.

03

Combine & Compete

Your best Wednesday net score plus both PGA players' 4-round stroke totals at the US Open combine into a single score (1 member score + 2 PGA scores). Lowest combined score wins. If a PGA pick misses the cut, rounds 3 and 4 are scored as 80 each.

Combined Score Formula
Best Wednesday Net Score
+ PGA Pick 1 Total Strokes (4 rounds) + PGA Pick 2 Total Strokes (4 rounds)
= Combined Score  (lowest wins)

Best Wednesday Net = your lower net score from Jun 17 or Jun 24 · Net Score = Gross strokes − course handicap

⚠

Missed Cut — PGA Player: If one of your PGA picks misses the cut (plays only 2 rounds at the US Open), their 4-round total is calculated as actual R1 + actual R2 + 80 + 80. An 80 is assigned for each unplayed round, giving every player a comparable 4-round total for scoring purposes.
